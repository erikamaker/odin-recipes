Thai-ish Fried Rice

Sweet, Spicy, Salty

I'm gluten-intolerant, so rice is a staple carb at my house. There are a lot of ways to dress the grain up, but the best I've found is a simple Thai(ish)-style fried recipe. The ingredients I use come from convenience. I usually have just enough of each ingredient to pull the dish together. Infused with fish sauce, soy sauce, heat, sugar, and spice-- it's by far my favorite dish. What do you think?

Ingredients I Guessed At

  • 2 Cups of Jasmine Rice (steamed and cooled)
  • 1 Clove of Garlic (minced)
  • 1 Medium Shallot (minced)
  • 1/2 cup of Chili Peppers (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup of Shredded Carrots
  • 1/4 cup of Scallions (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoon of Soy Sauce
  • 1 large chicken egg
  • Thai basil leaves to taste
  • 3 tablespoons of Sesame Oil
  • 1/2 tablespoon of Fish Sauce
  • 1/2 tablespoon of Sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of MSG
  • 2 tablespoons of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of white pepper

Recipe I Guessed At

  1. Steam rice and cool for 10 hours.
  2. Heat sesame oil and fish sauce over medium-high heat.
  3. Fry all minced ingredients and chili peppers in the oil.
  4. Scramble an egg in a corner of the work.
  5. After the egg is fried, fold rice into the mix.
  6. Bruise basil between fingers and toss in the rice.
  7. Fold carrots and basil into the rice.
  8. Add soy sauce. Continue to fry for about two minutes.
  9. Add salt, MSG, and white pepper. Toss around.
  10. Sprinkle sugar over dish and fry another two minutes.
  11. Garnish with scallions and serve (I think?) !
